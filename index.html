PINEAPPLE FRIED RICE RECIPE

Pineapple fried rice, known in Thailand as khao phat sapparot, is a popular Southeast Asian dish characterized by a sweet-and-savory flavor profile, combining jasmine rice, fresh pineapple chunks, cashews, and a savory sauce. Often served in a hollowed-out pineapple bowl, this dish typically features curry powder, fish sauce, and a mix of vegetables and proteins like shrimp or chicken.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Pineapple
  • Protein
  • Flavorings
  • Mix-ins/Garnish

Intsructions

  1. Cook jasmine rice ahead of time and cool it completely. If using a whole pineapple, cut it in half, score the inside, and scoop out the fruit to make a bowl.
  2. Sear shrimp or chicken in a wok with high heat, remove, and set aside. Scramble eggs and remove.
  3. Saut√© garlic, ginger, onion, and any veggies (peas, carrots, bell peppers).
  4. Add the rice and the sauce (fish sauce, soy sauce, curry powder) to the wok. Stir-fry on high heat until the rice is coated and slightly caramelized.
  5. Mix in the pineapple, cooked proteins, eggs, and cashews, tossing until heated through.
  6. Garnish with green onions and serve immediately, ideally in the pineapple bowl.

Tips For Success

Avoid Mushy Rice: Use cold, leftover rice. Balance the Flavors: Use a just-ripened, not over-ripe, pineapple to ensure it is sweet but not overly mushy, and balance the sweetness with fish sauce. High Heat: Cook on high heat to prevent the pineapple from releasing too much moisture and making the dish watery. Vegan Option: Substitute fish sauce with soy sauce or miso paste, and use tofu instead of meat.
